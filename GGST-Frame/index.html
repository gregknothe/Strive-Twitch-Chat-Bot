FAQ

Factors not accurately calculated:

Spacing

1. Projectile moves often do not hit during their first active frame in blockstrings due to pushback. Because of this the gap between a blocked move and projectile is often bigger than what the calculation would suggest. [Video example]
2. Advancing Moves are similar to projectiles as they are also moving hitboxes. Because of this, spacing often does affect the gap in blockstun. [Video example]
3. Faultless Defence allows the defender to push back the attacker on block for the cost of some meter. This mechanic changes the spacing of blockstrings, thus possibly changing the gap as well. [Video example]

Changing Hitboxes

1. Moving Hitboxes moves have hitboxes that "moves" (shocker, I know) throughout its active frames. Any factor changing the player's hurtbox can affect these moves, which include (but are not limited to): spacing, standing/crouching/airborne, character height, ect. [Video example]
2. Extending Hitboxes moves also change, but only in "size". The first few frames of a move can have a "1 Sol Badguy" unit length, but the next few frames it could extend to a "2 Sol Badguys" length. [Video example]

Jump-ins

1. Height of hit is the main factor when figuring out frame advantage from an aerial move. Rule of thumb, the higher you hit the opponent, the less frame advantage you will have. The site displays the blockstun assuming the hit is perfectly timed at the lowest height, which is not realistic (or even possible) for some moves. [Video example]
2. Landing recovery also plays a part in the calculations. Some moves allow you to act immediately after landing while others have a set amount of recovery after landing. It is kind of a mess to accurately calculate these, so you get hitstun instead. [Video example]

"Alternate" Versions and "N/A":

1. "Alternate" versions of moves are often denoted as "[x]". They can range from when a move's input is held down, Ky's Shock State debuff, when Ram is missing her sword, ect. Details of each present "alternate" move is (hopefully) detailed in the drop down "[character] Notes" section located below the move table. [Video example]
2. "N/A" is displayed when either the first move's blockstun cannot be calculated (ex. throws or moves without any blockstun) or if an error has occurred in my code causing it to crash. For the error part, please refer to the "Bug Reporting" section to send a bug report (if you want).
